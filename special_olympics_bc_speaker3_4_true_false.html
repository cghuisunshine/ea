Speaker 3 & Speaker 4 — True / False Practice Questions

Practice Goal

Use these six questions to review the main ideas from Speaker 3 and Speaker 4. Choose True or False, then check your answers.

Speaker 3: Inclusion in schools means real belonging, participation, choices, friendships, teamwork, and feeling valued.
Speaker 4: EAs support students by building independence, confidence, participation, and belonging.
Speaker 3
1

Inclusion means a student is simply placed in the same classroom or gym.

Correct answer: False. Real inclusion means the student participates, learns, makes choices, builds friendships, and feels valued.
Speaker 3
2

Programs such as Active Start and FUNdamentals help children develop movement skills, sport skills, social skills, and confidence.

Correct answer: True. These youth programs support children and teenagers with intellectual disabilities.
Speaker 3
3

Unified Sports separates students into “helpers” and “people who need help.”

Correct answer: False. Unified Sports brings students with and without intellectual disabilities together as teammates.
Speaker 3
4

In gym class, a teacher or EA can support inclusion by changing the pace, using a bigger ball, giving visual instructions, creating partner roles, or focusing on teamwork.

Correct answer: True. These are examples of adapting activities so more students can participate.
Speaker 4
5

The EA’s role is to do everything for the student.

Correct answer: False. The EA’s goal is to support independence, confidence, participation, and belonging.
Speaker 4
6

Inclusion is active: students need real chances to join, not just words saying they are welcome.

Correct answer: True. Speaker 4 says we need to create real chances for students to join.

Answer Key

  1. False — Real inclusion is more than being in the same place.
  2. True — Active Start and FUNdamentals build skills and confidence.
  3. False — Unified Sports makes everyone teammates.
  4. True — Adaptations can help students participate.
  5. False — EAs support independence, not dependence.
  6. True — Inclusion requires real chances to join.
