My understanding of emotions and behaviour has changed in a similar way. I once viewed strong emotions or challenging behaviour mainly as discipline problems. I now understand that emotional regulation develops gradually and that behaviour can be a form of communication.

A student may be communicating anxiety, frustration, confusion, sensory overload, or an unmet need. Calm adults can support regulation through clear routines, modelling, practice, co-regulation, and consistent feedback.

In my work as an EA, I want to ask, ‘What is this student trying to communicate?’ instead of focusing only on, ‘How can I stop this behaviour?’ This question will help me respond with greater patience and understanding.
