Now we will connect Special Olympics BC to the social model of disability .

The social model of disability says that disability is not only caused by a person’s impairment or diagnosis. Disability is also created by barriers in society.

These barriers can include low expectations, inaccessible programs, negative attitudes, lack of support, and being excluded from activities.

For example, if a student wants to join a game but the instructions are confusing, the rules are too fast, and nobody adapts the activity, the problem is not only inside the student. The problem is also in the environment.

Special Olympics BC helps remove these barriers. It gives people with intellectual disabilities real opportunities to train, compete, make friends, and be recognized for their strengths.

This contributes to rights because rights are not only about laws. Rights are also about real access, real participation, and real respect.

Special Olympics BC shows that people with intellectual disabilities have the right to participate in community life, to be included in sports, and to be proud of their achievements.

It also changes public attitudes. It helps society move from pity to respect, and from exclusion to inclusion.
