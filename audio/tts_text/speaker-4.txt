As future Education Assistants, we need to know about Special Olympics BC because EAs often support students who face barriers in school.

An EA may support a student in gym class, recess, field trips, clubs, classroom group work, or social activities. Sometimes students want to join, but they may feel nervous, confused, or left out.

An EA can help by breaking instructions into smaller steps, using visuals, modelling a skill, encouraging peer support, or helping the student manage anxiety.

The EA’s role is not to do everything for the student. The goal is to support independence, confidence, participation, and belonging.

Special Olympics BC also gives EAs a community resource to share with teachers and families. It shows that sport can be used to build friendship, leadership, health, and self-esteem.

Most importantly, it reminds us that inclusion is active. We need to create real chances for students to join, not just say they are welcome.
