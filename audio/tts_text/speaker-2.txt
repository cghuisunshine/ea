Special Olympics BC connects strongly to the social model of disability.

The social model says that disability is not only about a person’s diagnosis or impairment. Disability is also created by barriers in society. These barriers can include low expectations, inaccessible activities, negative attitudes, lack of support, and exclusion.

Special Olympics BC helps remove these barriers. It gives children and adults with intellectual disabilities real opportunities to train, compete, make friends, and be recognized for their strengths.

This contributes to disability rights because rights are not only about laws. Rights are also about real access, participation, dignity, and respect.

For example, if a person is never invited to join a sport, the barrier is not only inside the person. The barrier is also in the environment. But when the rules, coaching, and support are changed, that person can participate and succeed.

Special Olympics BC changes public attitudes. It helps people move from pity to respect, and from exclusion to inclusion.
