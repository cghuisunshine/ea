Good morning everyone. Today our group is presenting about Special Olympics BC .

Special Olympics BC is part of a larger movement that supports people with intellectual disabilities through sport, friendship, health, and community inclusion. This topic is important because it connects to disability rights, the social model of disability, and inclusion in schools.

Special Olympics did not begin only as a sports competition. It began because people started to challenge unfair beliefs about people with intellectual disabilities.

In the early 1960s, Dr. Frank Hayden, a Canadian researcher at the University of Toronto, studied children with intellectual disabilities at Beverley School in Toronto. At that time, many people believed these children could not be physically active or successful in sports.

Dr. Hayden’s research showed something very important. He found that many children with intellectual disabilities had low physical fitness not because they were unable, but because they had not been given enough chances to play, train, and participate.

This idea helped inspire the Special Olympics movement. Eunice Kennedy Shriver helped organize the first Special Olympics event in Chicago in 1968. Canada was represented at that first event, and later Harry “Red” Foster helped bring Special Olympics to Canada.

The first Canadian Special Olympics event was held in Toronto in 1969.
