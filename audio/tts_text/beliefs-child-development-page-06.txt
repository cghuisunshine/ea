This program has also changed how I think about identity. I now understand that children and adolescents develop their sense of self through family, culture, language, interests, school experiences, and feedback from other people. I had not previously considered how strongly an adult’s words could affect a child’s self-image. Repeatedly describing a child as lazy, difficult, or weak may influence how that child comes to see themselves.

As an EA, I want to notice each student’s strengths, use respectful language, and help every student feel accepted and capable. I also understand that nature and nurture continually influence each other. Biology may affect temperament, ability, or emotional responses, but relationships and experiences influence how these characteristics develop. A child’s future is not completely fixed.
