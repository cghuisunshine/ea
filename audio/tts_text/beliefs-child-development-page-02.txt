My earlier beliefs were shaped by my experiences in China. Before coming to Canada, I spent most of my career as a software engineer, and I also worked as a teacher for about two years. My own schooling and teaching took place in an education system that placed a strong emphasis on academic achievement and examination results.

Students were usually expected to learn at the same pace. When students could not keep up, they often had to solve the problem on their own or rely on family support. Because this was the system I knew, it strongly influenced how I first understood learning and success.
